<h2>Part 5: Negotiation Encounter — The Chainwarden Toll at Greypass</h2>

<p><em>Approximately Sólmánuður 10th. Day 5 of the journey.</em></p>

<h3>Context</h3>

<p>Where the Lantern Road enters the foothills of Midgard’s Shield, the Chainwardens maintain a fortified toll crossing called <strong>Greypass</strong>. It controls the only maintained road through the lower mountains. The toll was built to manage trade caravans; now it manages desperation.</p>

<div style="background:#2a2a3a; color:#e0d8c8; padding:14px 18px; margin:12px 0; border-radius:6px; border-left:4px solid #c4a03c; font-style:italic;">
<p>The road narrows between two bluffs of dark stone. A wall of stacked timber and iron bars blocks the path—not a military fortification, but close enough. A gate. A guardhouse. A flag bearing the Chainwarden sigil: a broken chain, re-forged.</p>

<p>Two wardens stand at the gate, wrapped in furs over mail. One holds a ledger. The other holds a spear.</p>

<p>"Papers," says the one with the ledger. It is not a request.</p>
</div>

<h3>The Encounter</h3>

<p>This is a <strong>Moderate Negotiation</strong> with the toll commander, <strong>Warden-Sergeant Eirik Greyhand</strong>.</p>

<p><strong>Motivations:</strong></p>

<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Eirik wants order.</strong> He is not corrupt—he is <strong>exhausted</strong>. The toll is underfunded, understaffed, and overwhelmed. He wants travellers to follow procedure so he can maintain the fiction that the system works.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Eirik wants supplies.</strong> The toll garrison hasn’t received a resupply in weeks. Heat (fuel, rations) is more valuable to him than coin.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Eirik wants to go home.</strong> He has a family in Hrafnborg. He hasn’t heard from them in a month. Any news—even bad news—is worth something.</p>
</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>What the PCs Want:</strong></p>

<ul>
<li>
<p>Passage through the toll without their cover being blown.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Ideally, intelligence about the road ahead (mountain conditions, bandit activity, undead sightings).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>If they have Bryn’s toll-token, they can present it for automatic free passage for one person. The rest must still negotiate.</p>
</li>
</ul>
