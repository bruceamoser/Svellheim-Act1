<h2>Part 8: Point of Interest — Kross-stead</h2>

<p>Place this on the mountain road, roughly Day 7–8.</p>

<div style="background:#2a2a3a; color:#e0d8c8; padding:14px 18px; margin:12px 0; border-radius:6px; border-left:4px solid #c4a03c; font-style:italic;">
<p>The road passes through what was once a mining settlement—a dozen stone buildings clustered around a shaft-head, with a crude smelter and a Chainwarden outpost. The outpost flag still flies, but there’s no one here.</p>

<p>The shaft-head is open. Warm air rises from the opening.</p>
</div>

<p>Kross-stead was a silver mine. The miners evacuated three weeks ago when the temperature in the lower shafts began <strong>rising</strong>—slowly, inexplicably. The Chainwarden garrison followed.</p>

<ul>
<li>
<p>If the PCs investigate the shaft (not recommended, but PCs are PCs): warm, sulphurous air. The walls of the shaft are veined with something that isn’t silver—a dark, root-like growth that pulses faintly with amber light. This is connected to Yggdrasil’s root system, though the PCs can’t know that yet. Anyone who touches the growth feels a vibration—like a heartbeat, very slow, very deep.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>If the PCs search the buildings: 1 day’s rations, a miner’s pickaxe (decent weapon), and a Chainwarden logbook. The last entry reads: <em>"The walls of the lower gallery are warm to the touch. Supervisor Alrek says it’s geothermal. Alrek is an idiot. Trees don’t grow underground."</em></p>
</li>
<li>
<p>If the PCs take rope, pitons, or climbing gear from the mining stores: +1 edge on one Agility test at Titan’s Stair.</p>
</li>
</ul>
