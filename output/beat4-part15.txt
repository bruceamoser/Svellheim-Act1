<h4>Ambush Aftermath</h4>

<p>If the PCs <strong>win</strong> the fight:</p>
<ul>
<li>Searching the barricade reveals the goblins' meagre supplies—stolen traveller's goods, dried cave-fish, and corroded coins. Worth 10 gold total.</li>
<li>A crude map scratched on cave-bark shows the goblin caves deeper in the Titanspine, with an area marked in charcoal scratches. An easy Intuition test identifies this as the direction of Hrafnborg—the goblins were fleeing <em>from</em> that direction.</li>
<li>This is key intelligence: something in the southwest is driving creatures—living and dead—out of their homes.</li>
<li>Earn <strong>1 Victory</strong> for defeating the ambush.</li>
</ul>

<p>If the PCs <strong>negotiate</strong>:</p>
<ul>
<li>They lose half their remaining rations but gain the crude map (same intelligence as above).</li>
<li>The Monarch offers a “gift”—a tarnished bronze ring with a rune that neither the Monarch nor the PCs can read. (This is an ancient Jötunfolk traveller's ring. It's worth nothing, but it has sentimental value to the Monarch. Accepting it graciously earns +1 to future interactions with non-hostile goblin groups.)</li>
</ul>

<p>If the PCs <strong>flee</strong>:</p>
<ul>
<li>The goblins take their supplies from wherever the PCs were camped or resting. Lose all remaining rations.</li>
<li>The goblins don't pursue beyond their territory.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Part 11: Arrival at Stairwatch</h2>

<p><em>Approximately Sólmánuður 15th–16th.</em></p>

<p>Regardless of montage outcomes, the PCs arrive at Stairwatch.</p>

<div style="background:#2a2a3a; color:#e0d8c8; padding:14px 18px; margin:12px 0; border-radius:6px; border-left:4px solid #c4a03c; font-style:italic;">
<p>Stairwatch hunches at the foot of Titan’s Stair like a dog that’s been kicked too many times. It’s a waystation town—built for travellers who never stay long, and it shows. The buildings lean. The streets are mud frozen into ridges. Smoke rises from every chimney, but the fires burn low.</p>

<p>The inn-sign reads <em>The Broken Stair</em>. Below it, smaller: <em>Rooms. Ale. No questions.</em></p>

<p>The town’s Ever-Ember burns in a stone ring at the central crossroads. It’s the smallest you’ve seen—barely a campfire. But it burns. It burns.</p>
</div>
