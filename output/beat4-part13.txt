<p>These are <strong>travel-cairns</strong>—monuments left by generations of travellers who survived the crossing. The tradition: add a stone to the pile, speak a name, and the crossing grants safe passage.</p>

<p><em>This encounter triggers on a Total Failure or at the Director's discretion after a Partial Success. Goblins—thought extinct in the Titanspine for generations—have emerged from deep mountain caves, driven forward by an unknown power. They are desperate, feral, and organised under a self-styled monarch.</em></p>

<div style="background:#2a2a3a; color:#e0d8c8; padding:14px 18px; margin:12px 0; border-radius:6px; border-left:4px solid #c4a03c; font-style:italic;">
<p>The descent path switchbacks through a narrow defile—loose scree above, a sheer drop below. Ahead, the trail is blocked by a crude barricade of broken cart wheels and piled stones.</p>

<p>Then the chittering starts.</p>

<p>Small shapes scramble along the cliff walls above you. Green-grey skin, yellow eyes, crude weapons. One of them—slightly larger, wearing a battered crown made from bent nails and copper wire—stands on a boulder and shrieks something in a language you don't understand.</p>

<p>Goblins. Here. That shouldn't be possible.</p>
