<h3>Round 2 Interlude: The Drowned Village</h3>
<div style="background:#2a2a3a; color:#e0d8c8; padding:14px 18px; margin:12px 0; border-radius:6px; border-left:4px solid #c4a03c; font-style:italic;">
<p>The Lantern Road crosses what was once a shallow lake—you can tell by the reed-stumps and the change in ground texture. At the lake’s centre, barely visible through frost-haze, rooftops jut from the ice. A village, flooded and frozen.</p>

<p>The ice is clear enough to see through in places. Below: a market square, complete with stalls and carts. A temple spire, its bell still visible. Streets and doorways and—</p>

<p>Movement. Something shifts below the ice. Not a fish. Something with <strong>hands</strong>.</p>
</div>

<p>The PCs should <strong>not</strong> investigate. If they do: the ice creaks ominously, and whatever is below tracks their movement across the surface. A easy Reason test identifies this as a draugr nest—the village died and the residents didn’t leave. Breaking the ice would release dozens of them.</p>

<p>If the PCs are wise enough to move on, they gain useful information: the dead are <strong>everywhere</strong>, not just in obvious battlefields and barrows. The Frostfen is a cemetery that doesn’t know it’s dead.</p>
